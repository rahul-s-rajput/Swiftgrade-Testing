f(x) = 3x³ - 2x² + 5x - 7

f'(x) = d/dx(3x³) - d/dx(2x²) + d/dx(5x) - d/dx(7)

Q3. (12 marks) Explain the concept of photosynthesis and write the chemical equation.
Photosynthesis is the process by which green plants use sunlight to synthesize nutrients from carbon dioxide and water. It occurs in the chloroplasts of plant cells, specifically in the chlorophyll molecules.

The process converts light energy into chemical energy stored in glucose. Oxygen is released as a byproduct.

Chemical Equation:
6CO₂ + 6H₂O + light energy → C₆H₁₂O₆ + 6O₂
(carbon dioxide + water + light → glucose + oxygen)
This process is essential for life on Earth as it provides oxygen and is the primary source of energy for most ecosystems.

Q5. (10 marks) Balance the following chemical equation: Fe + O₂ → Fe₂O₃
Unbalanced: Fe + O₂ → Fe₂O₃

Step 1: Count atoms on each side
Left: Fe = 1, O = 2
Right: Fe = 2, O = 3

Step 2: Balance Fe first
2Fe + O₂ → Fe₂O₃

Step 3: Balance O
Need 3 O on left, but O₂ gives 2
Use fractions: 2Fe + (3/2)O₂ → Fe₂O₃

Step 4: Clear fractions by multiplying by 2
4Fe + 3O₂ → 2Fe₂O₃

Answer: 4Fe + 3O₂ → 2Fe₂O₃
Q6. (15 marks) A ball is thrown upward with initial velocity 20 m/s. Find maximum height and time to reach it.

Q7. (10 marks) Describe the structure of an atom and explain atomic number and mass number.
An atom consists of three main particles:

1. Nucleus (center):
- Contains protons (positive charge)
- Contains neutrons (no charge)
- Very small but contains most of the atom's mass

2. Electrons:
- Negative charge
- Orbit the nucleus in energy levels/shells
- Much lighter than protons and neutrons

[Simple diagram showing nucleus with p+ and n, and electron shells with e-]
Atomic Number (Z): Number of protons in the nucleus. This defines the element.
Example: Carbon has 6 protons, so Z = 6

Mass Number (A): Total number of protons + neutrons
Example: Carbon-12 has 6 protons + 6 neutrons = 12
A = Z + N (where N = number of neutrons)

Q10. (5 marks) State Newton's Second Law of Motion and give its mathematical form.
Newton's Second Law of Motion:

"The acceleration of an object is directly proportional to the net force acting on it and inversely proportional to its mass."

In other words, when a force acts on an object, it causes the object to accelerate. The larger the force, the greater the acceleration. The larger the mass, the smaller the acceleration.

Mathematical Form:
F = ma

Where:
F = Net force (in Newtons, N)
m = Mass (in kilograms, kg)
a = Acceleration (in m/s²)
